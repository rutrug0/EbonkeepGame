# Product Pillars and Scope

## Product Vision
Ebonkeep is a web-first, session-flexible fantasy RPG focused on progression loops, equipment hunting, and tactical preparation. It supports both active play and passive progress windows.

## Audience
- Fans of browser RPG progression loops (Gladiatus/Shakes & Fidget style pacing).
- Players who prefer medium-session strategy over real-time action pressure.
- Desktop and mobile users who can play in short bursts.

## Pillars
1. Persistent progression: every session should produce meaningful account growth.
2. Tactical preparation over twitch input: class/build/loadout decisions matter more than reflexes.
3. Readable economy: clear currencies, sinks, and timers.
4. Broad-appeal dark fantasy: mature atmosphere without niche visual harshness.
5. Optional intensity: manual input improves efficiency but automation prevents dead sessions.

## MVP In Scope (v1)
- Account onboarding via platform login.
- Classes: Warrior, Wizard, Archer.
- Level progression + gear score progression.
- PvE combat encounters (solo and group-enemy variants).
- Inventory grid management and equipment slots.
- Itemization: base items, rarity, affixes, consumables.
- Contracts board (timed activity progression).
- Instanced shops with refresh timers.
- Hybrid pacing controls: stamina + timer activities.
- Basic live-ops event framework (biweekly cadence support).

## Explicitly Out of Scope (v1)
- Full clan warfare implementation.
- Full PvP ladders/seasons.
- Campaign-style authored narrative progression.
- Crafting professions and player market.
- Real-money auction house.

## Success Criteria (Planning Baseline)
- New player reaches first meaningful gear decision within first 20 minutes.
- Core loop can be understood with no external guide.
- No pay-to-win direct power sales in v1 monetization.
- Systems support growth to target 500 peak CCU without architecture rewrite.

## Non-Goals
- Hyper-casual idle-only gameplay.
- Action-combat mechanics with direct movement aiming.
- Heavy cinematic storytelling as a retention driver.
