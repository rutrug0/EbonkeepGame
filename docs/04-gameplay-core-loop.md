# Gameplay Core Loop

## Primary Loop
1. Claim completed timers (contracts board/shop refreshes/rewards).
2. Prepare loadout (gear, consumables, inventory moves).
3. Spend stamina on PvE encounters.
4. Resolve combat, receive loot/currency/XP.
5. Evaluate upgrades and sell/salvage extras.
6. Start new timed activities and log out or continue.

## Secondary Loops
- Economy loop: buy low-value upgrades, sell excess gear, plan next refresh.
- Inventory loop: optimize grid space and stash usage.
- Consumable loop: time boosts for high-yield sessions.

## Session Archetypes
- 5-minute session:
  - collect timers, quick inventory cleanup, one short mission.
- 15-minute session:
  - multiple missions, shop visit, equipment optimization, refresh contracts.
- 30+ minute session:
  - focused grind window with consumables and target farming.
  - stat optimization on gear

## Core Activities
### PvE Missions
- Cost stamina.
- Produce XP, currencies, and item drops.
- Difficulty scaling by level bracket and gear score envelope.

### Contracts Board
- Timed activity board with up to 6 available contracts at a time.
- Contracts expire if not accepted before their timer ends.
- Expired or abandoned contracts replenish after a 1-2 hour cooldown.

### Instanced Shops
- Player-personal stock.
- Refreshes every fixed duration.
- Includes gold items and occasional premium-currency offers.

### Inventory Management
- Grid-based storage with item size constraints.
- Equip/swap/sell/salvage actions.
- Stash used for overflow and long-term item retention.

## Friction and Pacing Controls
- Stamina limits burst progression.
- Timers shape session return points.
- Consumables create planned efficiency windows.
- Shops create periodic decision pressure.

## Reward Cadence Principles
- Every mission yields at least one meaningful output (XP, item, or currency).
- Small upgrades should appear frequently.
- Large power spikes should be rare but visible.
